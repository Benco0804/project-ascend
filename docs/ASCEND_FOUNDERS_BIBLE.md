# Project Ascend – Founder's Bible

## 1. Vision

Project Ascend helps people build a healthier lifestyle through small daily habits, gamified progress, adaptive goals, and AI coaching.

The product is not a calorie tracker.
The product is not a fitness app.
The product is a habit-building system for healthy living.

---

## 2. Product Thesis

People do not fail because they lack information.
They fail because consistency is hard.

Most health products focus on tracking outcomes:
- weight
- calories
- workouts
- macros

Ascend focuses on reinforcing actions:
- showing up
- completing small quests
- building identity
- improving one habit at a time

The core belief:
Healthy people are built through repeated small actions, not perfect plans.

---

## 3. Product Principles

1. Reward consistency, not perfection.
2. Reward actions, not outcomes.
3. Focus on healthy lifestyle habits, not only weight loss.
4. One small habit at a time.
5. The app should make users want to come back tomorrow.
6. AI should act as a coach, not a judge.
7. No feature enters MVP unless it improves retention or habit formation.

---

## 4. Target User

Initial user:
A person who wants to become healthier but does not want to count calories, weigh food, or follow a strict fitness plan.

They want progress.
They want structure.
They want accountability.
They want to feel like they are becoming a healthier version of themselves.

---

## 5. Core Problem

Most people know what they should do:
- move more
- sleep better
- eat better
- drink water
- train consistently

But they struggle to do it consistently.

The problem is not lack of knowledge.
The problem is lack of daily reinforcement, adaptive guidance, and emotional reward.

---

## 6. Core Loop

1. User opens the app.
2. User sees today’s quest.
3. User completes a small healthy action.
4. User gets progress feedback.
5. User builds streak / XP / identity progress.
6. App adapts the next quest.
7. User returns tomorrow.

---

## 7. MVP Scope

The MVP should include only:

- Simple onboarding
- Daily quest
- Habit check-in
- XP / streak / level
- Adaptive next mission
- Weekly AI review
- Basic progress screen

---

## 8. Not in MVP

These features are intentionally excluded from the first version:

- Calorie tracking
- Food database
- Barcode scanner
- Full workout builder
- Social feed
- Leaderboards
- Complex coach dashboard
- Marketplace
- Wearable integrations

---

## 9. Open Questions

- What is the first habit category we should start with?
- Should the user choose the first goal, or should the app recommend it?
- How much AI should appear in the daily experience?
- What is the minimum gamification layer needed for retention?
- Should weight be optional, secondary, or completely hidden?
- What does “success” mean in the first 30 days?
